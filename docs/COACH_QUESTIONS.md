# Questions for the Coach
### Extracting the highest-value methodology (internal prep)

Goal of the conversation: find out **how he thinks**, turn it into the rules/numbers the app already has slots for, and gauge whether he's a partner. Let him talk — coaches love explaining their system, and the gold is in the *why*, not just the numbers.

Order matters: open with respect for his result, get him explaining his system, *then* get specific. Don't lead with business.

---

## 0 · Open (build rapport, set the frame)
- "Walk me through how you took [athlete] from wherever she started to world champion — what were the actual levers?"
- "When a new athlete walks in, what are the first things you measure or watch?"
- "What do you think most HYROX apps and coaches get *wrong*?"

## 1 · Diagnosis — how he reads an athlete
*(maps to our limiter/quadrant engine)*
- How do you decide what's actually limiting someone — is it run, strength, stations, engine, pacing, mobility?
- What numbers or tests do you trust for that? What do you ignore?
- Two athletes with the same finish time — how do their training needs differ?
- Where does **mobility** sit for you — limiter, injury risk, or afterthought?

## 2 · Strength standards
*(replaces our division strength grid)*
- For each division (Open/Pro, men's/women's): what lift numbers mean strength is **no longer** the limiter? (squat / deadlift / bench, or whatever you use — absolute or bodyweight multiple)
- Which lifts actually predict HYROX performance, and which don't matter?
- How does strength training change across a prep — and when do you cap it?

## 3 · The stations
*(replaces our station-time benchmarks → makes goal-pace math real)*
- What are realistic **station split times** for, say, a sub-1:10 vs 1:20 vs 1:30 athlete? Per station if you have it.
- Which stations bleed the most time for most people? Which are most trainable?
- How do you train station capacity *under fatigue* vs fresh?
- Roxzone / transitions — how much time is hiding there, and how do you train it?

## 4 · Running
*(replaces our pace model)*
- How do you set HYROX run pace off a 5K (or whatever you use)? Is 5K even the right input?
- How much slower is race run pace vs fresh 5K, in your experience?
- How do you build "compromised running" — running well *off* the stations?

## 5 · Periodization & sessions
*(replaces our phase structure + session generator)*
- How do you structure a prep — phases, how long each, how they shift by time-to-race?
- A typical training **week** in build vs peak — what's in it?
- Your highest-leverage sessions — the ones that actually move the needle?
- How do you taper for HYROX specifically?
- How do you adjust the plan when life/fatigue gets in the way?

## 6 · Recovery & readiness
*(maps to our readiness model)*
- How do you decide when an athlete should back off vs push?
- Do you use HRV / resting HR / sleep? How much do you trust them vs how they *feel*?
- What are your early warning signs of overtraining or illness?

## 7 · Nutrition / weight / body comp *(if he goes there)*
- How much does bodyweight / power-to-weight matter for HYROX, and how do you manage it in a prep?

## 8 · What's missing in the market
- If you could wave a wand and have software do one thing for you and your athletes, what is it?
- What would make you actually *use* this with your athletes day to day?
- What would make you trust an app's recommendation enough to not override it?

---

## Business / partnership (only once he's leaning in)
- "What does involvement look like to you — your method in the app, a coaching tool for your gym, a brand you're part of?"
- Listen for whether he wants: **licensing** his method, **equity/co-founder**, a **gym tool**, or just to be associated. Each is a very different deal.
- **Decide your floor before you walk in.** You built the product; his methodology is content. A clean deal keeps those separate.
- Don't hand over a build or the codebase until there's a written understanding (even a simple mutual NDA). A live demo on your phone is fine; a sendable build is not.

## Two things to watch for
- **Signal he's serious:** he offers specifics (real numbers, real session structure) unprompted, and asks about *his* athletes using it.
- **Signal to slow down:** he wants ownership/control disproportionate to contribution, or wants the IP/code before any agreement.
