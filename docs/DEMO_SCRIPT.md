# Live Demo Script
### The 5-beat walkthrough for the coach meeting (~5 minutes)

Demo on **your own account** — it's full of real data, which is far more convincing than an empty fresh user. Save the onboarding flow for the end as "here's how I'd set up *your* athletes."

---

## Before you walk in (2-min checklist)
- [ ] Phone **unlocked**, Do Not Disturb on, brightness up.
- [ ] You're on **"Your data"** mode (App info → Developer says *Your data*, not Sandbox).
- [ ] Open the app and **pull-to-refresh on Today** so readiness + last workout are current.
- [ ] **Network on** (the AI coach needs it).
- [ ] Have **one coach question** ready to type (see Beat 4).
- [ ] Know your headline numbers cold: your limiter, your readiness today, your goal/date.

---

## Beat 1 — Today (the daily hook) · ~45s
**Tap:** Today tab.
**Show:** the readiness number, the recovery readout (HRV vs your own normal band), today's next session.
**Say:**
> "Every morning I open this and get one honest number — how ready I am today. It's read straight from my Apple Health — heart-rate variability, resting HR, sleep — but scored against *my own* baseline, not a generic chart. If I trained hard yesterday or I'm getting sick, it knows before I do."

*Point at the HRV line:* "See — it shows the actual number and my normal range, so it's not a black box."

## Beat 2 — Athlete (the diagnosis — the wow) · ~60s
**Tap:** Athlete tab.
**Show:** the quadrant with the YOU marker, "the read" (limiter/focus), focus-metric goals, trend charts.
**Say:**
> "This is the part I think you'll care about. It placed me on the HYROX map from my real numbers — my 5K, my lifts measured against *my division's* standards. It says my limiter is [your limiter]. It's not guessing from a quiz — it's measuring."

*Scroll to the charts:* "And it's tracking the trends that matter — form, HRV, training load — reconstructed from my history."

## Beat 3 — Plan (the depth) · ~60s
**Tap:** Plan tab.
**Show:** the "Phases to race day" roadmap, then scroll the session timeline.
**Say:**
> "From my race date and my limiter, it built a full periodized plan — base, build, peak, taper — and filled in every week to race day. And these aren't 'go for a run' — look: run paces calculated from my goal finish time, and station work at the exact weights I'll race."

*This is the natural segue:* "And this is exactly where your methodology would go — these phase lengths, the sessions, the standards are my best version; yours would make them world-class."

## Beat 4 — AI Coach (the magic) · ~60s
**Tap:** AI Coach tab. **Type a real question**, e.g.:
- *"Is my [goal] finish realistic by [date]?"* — or —
- *"I feel wrecked today — what should I do?"* — or —
- *"Rebuild this week around more strength."*

**Say (while it streams):**
> "And I can just ask it. It's reasoning over everything you've seen — my diagnosis, my readiness, my training load, my plan. And it doesn't just talk —"

*If you used "rebuild my week":* "— watch, it actually changed my plan." *(flip to Plan tab to show the COACH-tagged sessions)*

> "It also won't make a call off stale data. If it doesn't trust the numbers, it tells you instead of bluffing."

## Beat 5 — Onboarding (how his athletes get set up) · ~45s
**Tap:** menu → App info → Developer → **Experience as a new user** → walk the first couple of steps and land on the **reveal**.
**Say:**
> "And this is how I'd onboard your athletes — a couple of honest questions, connect Apple Health, and it places them on the map and builds their plan in about a minute. Imagine every athlete who walks into your gym getting this, running your method."

*(Then App info → Restore my data to get your real account back.)*

---

## The close
> "The app's the delivery system — the diagnosis, the daily readiness, the plan, the coach all work. What it doesn't have yet is *your* methodology. Right now those numbers are my best guess. I'd love to make them yours."

Then **stop talking and listen.** Let him react.

---

## If something goes wrong
- **Coach won't respond** → "it needs signal — let me show you the plan it already built instead" → Beat 3. Don't fight it live.
- **Readiness looks stale** → pull-to-refresh, or just say "it flags when data's stale — that's the point, it won't bluff."
- **He grabs the phone and starts poking** → great sign. Let him. Answer questions, don't recite the script.
- **He asks 'can it do X?'** and it can't → "not yet — that's exactly the kind of thing I'd build with you."

## One rule
Don't oversell what's heuristic. When he asks where a number comes from, the honest answer — *"that's my reasoned default; yours would replace it"* — is the most persuasive thing you can say. It tells him there's a real seat at the table.
